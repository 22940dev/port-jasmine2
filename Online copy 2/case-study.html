Hike It
Overview

Hike It is a travel app I created for people visiting Northern Ireland.

Skills
  • Research
  • Sketching
  • Wireframes
  • UI/UX Design
Tools
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe Photoshop
Introduction

For this project I created a travel app called “Hike It” for people visiting Northern Ireland.

Northern Ireland has stunning scenery with deep mountain valleys, sharp cliffs that tower high above the sea, as well as cultural cities.

Therefore, I picked out three locations to highlight in my app. The three hikes are Cuilcagh, Cave Hill and The Mourne Mountains. I also have 5 screens: Launch Screen, Category and Description screen 1-3.

The Case

I spent some time in the beginning looking at hiking guides and other people’s travel experiences to gain a broad knowledge on important information such as average durability, difficulty, etc.

Because let’s face it. Little is worse than hiking a six-hour long trail which specifically says one to two hours on a map/app.

I then moved onto paper sketching and having hiked all locations previously was helpful when it came to designing content and illustrations. For my logo and three hikes, I took inspiration from personal photographs and simple graphic elements on Dribbble and Behance.

When researching iconography for my important information section, I found the website The Noun Project really inspiring when it came to designing my own icons.

Obstacles that I faced during this project was for example that I was very new to Adobe Illustrator at the time, so it took me a lot of time and effort to digitalize the illustrations. And with that in mind, I could have toned down the level of detail and simplified the illustrations. I also wish I spent more time aligning my illustrations to make them more consistent.

Summary

All in all, I’m happy with the outcome, and despite strict time constraints and unfamiliar software, I was able to create numerous sketches and layouts before digitalizing them in Adobe Illustrator, which I now will say I’m very familiar with.
