Introduction

First of all, what is a monogram, visual marque and wordmark?
A monogram is usually a design consisting of two or more letters to form a symbol. A visual marque is often used as a visual element of a brand and a wordmark is usually a text-only element of a brand.


This project was in many ways an enjoyable and valuable learning experience when it comes to brand image. I had fun developing my brand elements and finding my own aesthetic values, all while learning about logos, visual elements and language.

The Case

I spent the first few weeks researching famous and aesthetically pleasing monograms such as Calvin Klein, Gucci, Chanel, Louis Vuitton and many more. I was particularly drawn to sleek shapes and modern touches.

I also drew numerous wordmarks, visual marques and especially monograms which I narrowed down to a few I liked.

After creating a monogram based on my initials G and E, the branding process became a little easier as I could use my monogram to create nice contrasts when designing a visual marque and wordmark.

I began researching famous visual marques such as Airbnb, Dropbox, Instagram and many more.

What struck me early though is that my first name is Gro, a Scandinavian name that literally means to grow. I am also growing as a designer, so it felt natural for me to involve growth and progress in some way.

I then experimented with roots, plants and earth tones which eventually led to my leaf-circled visual marque.

I then began to explore wordmarks and their important role as part of a considered brand identity. After researching numerous typefaces and fonts on websites such as Typewolf, Google Fonts, Behance and etc. I became especially fond by the Sans-serif typeface and fonts like Avenir Next, Helvetica and Simplifica.

I ended up choosing Avenir Next for my wordmark because it is minimal, futuristic and professional like my monogram and visual marque.

Obstacles I faced during this project was for example that I felt I was being too much of a perfectionist in a way that I tried to improve and perfect every little detail, which turned out to be be very stressful towards the end of my deadline. I overcame this obstacle by realising that designs can always be reworked and refined, and I have been introduced to many work techniques through this course, which has helped me a lot. Like the saying goes, work smarter, not harder. The idea of building a brand identity was also very intimidating but the power of step-by-step is strong so the design process was not as bad as it seemed to be.

Summary

All in all, I am happy with the result, and I think the monogram, visual marque and wordmark goes well together. They do not fight for attention and that is something I had in mind from the very start.
